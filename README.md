📊 HR Employee Attrition Analysis

🔍 Overview

Understanding employee attrition is critical for improving retention, productivity, and organizational stability.
This project analyzes HR data to uncover patterns in workforce distribution, salary structure, experience, and demographics.

🎯 Objective
- Analyze employee distribution across departments
- Identify salary trends by role and education
- Evaluate experience levels across teams
- Explore gender and age patterns
- Provide actionable insights to reduce attrition

📁 Dataset
- Source: HR Employee Attrition Dataset
- Total Records: 1470 employees
- Key Features:
             - Age
             - Department
             - Job Role
             - Monthly Income
             - Education
             - Gender
             - Years at Company
             - Attrition

🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- SQL (SQLite) – Data analysis & querying
-  PPT – Presentation & storytelling
- Data Analysis Techniques – Aggregations, Grouping, Sorting

📊 Key Business Questions & Insights

1️⃣ Total Employees

1470 employees

💡 Insight:
A solid dataset size enables reliable trend analysis across departments.

2️⃣ Employees by Department
R&D → 961
Sales → 446
HR → 63

💡 Insight:
R&D dominates the workforce (~65%), while HR has minimal representation, indicating potential operational strain.

3️⃣ Average Age by Department
HR → 37.81
R&D → 37.04
Sales → 36.54

💡 Insight:
Balanced age distribution suggests no major demographic skew across departments.

4️⃣ Highest Paying Job Role

Manager (~17K avg income)

💡 Insight:
Clear salary hierarchy with leadership roles earning significantly higher.

5️⃣ Gender Distribution
R&D → Male-dominant
Sales → Moderately balanced
HR → More balanced

💡 Insight:
Opportunity to improve gender diversity in technical roles.

6️⃣ Income by Education Level
Level 5 → Highest (~8277)
Level 1 → Lowest (~5640)

💡 Insight:
Higher education strongly correlates with higher income.

7️⃣ Most Experienced Department

Sales (~7.28 years)

💡 Insight:
Sales shows strong retention and experience stability compared to other departments.

📈 Key Takeaways
- R&D is the core department but may face scaling challenges
- Salary is strongly influenced by education and role
- Sales has the most experienced workforce
- Gender imbalance exists in technical teams
- HR team size is relatively small for the organization

🚀 Recommendations
- Improve gender diversity in R&D
- Expand HR team capacity
- Invest in employee upskilling programs
- Strengthen retention strategies in R&D
- Build clear career progression paths

📌 Conclusion

This analysis highlights critical workforce trends in distribution, compensation, and experience.
By leveraging these insights, organizations can make data-driven HR decisions to enhance employee satisfaction, reduce attrition, and improve overall efficiency.
